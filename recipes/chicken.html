Chicken Fajitas



This recipe is my own take on chicken fajitas, and can easily be made to feed a family, or leave enough for leftovers. Instead of corn, this recipe uses flour tortillas since they are less likely to tear apart when actually making the fajita. Be careful when cooking chicken! If improperly cooked or undercooked, it may lead to food poisoning. Always cook chicken to at least 165 F or about 74 Celcius.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb of chicken thighs or breast
  • 1 can of Black Beans
  • 1/2 Lime
  • 1 teaspoon of Salt
  • 1 teaspoon of Pepper
  • 1 teaspoon of Cayenne
  • 1/4 cup of shredded or grated cheese of choice [I used cheddar]
  • 1/4 cup of Jalapenos
  • 1 Bell Pepper, diced
  • 1 medium onion, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of butter
  • 4 flour tortillas


Instructions:

  1. Dice the bell pepper, and mince the onion, and season the chicken. Squeeze some lime juice onto the chicken, and once mixed, cut the chicken into small chunks.
  2. Put a pan on meidum to high heat, grease the pan with butter, and cook the chicken for about 15 to 20 minutes on each side, or until it reaches a temperature of at least 165 F
  3. Once the chicken is cooked, take off the pan and set aside to rest. After, reduce the heat to medium and put in the onion and Pepper
  4. Once the onions have lightly caramelized [Once they have turned lightly brown], toss in the jalapenos, black beans and add the chicken back in.
  5. Once everything has been cooked, grab your tortillas, fill, spinkle cheese on top, and they're ready to eat!
