Meatballs



These meatballs can be quickly formed, and baked for an easy dinner. While I used turkey, any ground meat can easily be subsituted. This recipe doesn't use breadcrumbs like most meatball recipes do, however, the inclusion of an egg is necessary to prevent the meatballs from falling apart while they bake.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb of ground meat of choice
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 1/2 teaspoon of oregano
  • 1 1/4 teaspoon of ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon of pepper
  • A pinch of salt
  • 1 can of crushed tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup of red wine vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon of parsley

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 420 F, line a tray or baking sheet with parchement paper
  2. Gather meat of choice with spices, seasonings and egg into a bowl and mix well, then form into balls
  3. Shape the meat into balls about 1 to 2 inches in size, or to your liking
  4. After all of the meatballs have been formed, place the meatballs onto a parchment lined baking sheet and bake for 20 to 25 minutes
  5. In the meantime, place a pan on medium heat.
  6. Add in the can of crushed tomatoes, alongside the red wine vinegar, and occosionally stirring
  7. Once the meatballs have been fully baked, remove any excess grease or fat that pools around them, and coat them in the sauce. They're now ready to eat!
