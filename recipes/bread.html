Protein Bread

Boring life story for how I made this recipe...

Aside from the addition of protein powder and usage of greek yogurt, this recipe is made to imitate a typical yeast bread recipe. However, the addition of these 2 ingredients can lead to a dense bread, making it vital to allow the bread to proof to counteract this. I make this bread to serve as a filling snack to help fulfill my daily protein needs when exercising.

Ingredients:
  • 1 Scoop of Protein Powder of choice
  • 1 1/2 teaspoon Yeast
  • 1/4 cup of Water
  • 3/4 cup of Greek Yogurt
  • 3 1/2 cup of Bread Flour
  • 1/4 cup of Milk, warm
  • 1 tablespoon of Salt
  • 2 teaspoons of Cinnamon

Instructions
  1. In a large bowl, add the milk and yeast then stir. Afterwards, let the mixture sit for ~10 minutes.
  2. Once the yeast had bloomed, add the rest of the liquid ingredients and set aside
  3. In a separate bowl, add the dry ingredients together and whisk to remove any clumps. Afterwards, add to the liquid mixture and incorporate everything
  4. Once everything has been mixed, knead the bread on a floured surface until the dough is stretchy and holds it's form
  5. After kneading, let the dough sit and proof for at least 1 hour, or up to 2 hours. This lets the bread dough rise in volume as the gases expand from the yeast activation.
  6. 20 minutes before you bread is done proofing, preheat an oven to 350 F, and afterwards, cook for 12 to 15 minutes.
  7. Enjoy!
